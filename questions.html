Question to Address

The topics I hope to address can be divided into the following bins:

Ethics

Is PGD ethically permissible? How much could it reduce suffering? Is selecting against an embryo with a particular disorder (say, Down Syndrome) an implicit devaluation of the lives of those who have that disorder?

Population and Medical Genetics

How would PGD work in practice? How useful are PGS for predicting disease? (These are the nitty-gritty questions I hope to dig into.)

Evolutionary Biology

A priori, is it feasible to select against disease traits without causing adverse consequences? Can we perturb a complex, multidimensional system like the human genome without throwing everything out of whack? S hould we heed the precautionary principle?

Embryology and Reproductive Medicine

How difficult is PGD from the perspective of reproductive medicine? What is in-vitro fertilization like for a mother, both literally and emotionally? What percentage of embryos implant successfully and how does this vary with maternal and paternal age?

Economics

How useful would PGD be from a cost-benefit perspective? How much would it lower healthcare costs?

Public Opinion and Policy

What does the public think of PGD? What percentage of prospective mothers would be willing to undergo IVF + PGD?

Synthetic Biology

How far away are we from chromosome, gamete, or whole embryo synthesis? What about iterated embryo selection?
